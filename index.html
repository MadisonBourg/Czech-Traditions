Vánočka

A Beloved Czech Christmas Bread

What Makes Vánočka Special

Vánočka is more than just bread—it’s a tradition steeped in love and memory. For me, it represents the warmth of winter holidays, the closeness of family and friends, and the joy of cherished moments. My grandmother makes Vánočka every Christmas, and it has become a comforting centerpiece of our celebrations.

The Taste of Tradition

Slightly sweet, with a soft, tender center and a beautifully golden braided crust, Vánočka is delicious and visually inviting. Whether made plain or with dried fruits and almonds, it offers a customizable experience perfect for sharing with large groups.

A Bread That Tells a Story

Every slice of Vánočka carries the story of holidays past: the laughter in the kitchen, the scent of baking bread, and the sparkle of snow outside. It’s not just food—it’s a feeling. And that feeling is what I treasure most.

Vánočka Recipe

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2/3 cup warm milk
  • 2 eggs + 1 egg for brushing
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Optional: raisins, chopped almonds, lemon zest

Instructions

  1. Activate yeast in warm milk with a bit of sugar. Let sit for 10 mins.
  2. Mix flour, sugar, salt, eggs, butter, vanilla, and yeast mixture until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead for 10 minutes. Cover and let rise in a warm place for 1–2 hours.
  4. Divide into strands and braid. Optional: add raisins or almonds between layers.
  5. Place on a baking sheet, let rise again for 30–45 minutes.
  6. Brush with beaten egg and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 35–40 minutes until golden brown.
  7. Cool slightly, dust with powdered sugar, and enjoy!
Made with love to celebrate tradition, family, and the joy of Christmas.
